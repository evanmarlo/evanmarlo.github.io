GASPACS

The Get Away Special Passive Attitude Control Satellite (GASPACS) was a 1U CubeSat created by Utah State University's Get Away Special undergraduate research team. I joined the team in Fall 2020 and was hired as a research fellow in the Summer of 2021 to help complete the satellite.

Mission

The mission's primary objective was to deploy an inflatable boom in low Earth orbit and transmit a clear picture back. The mission's secondary objective was to determine the effectiveness of the boom in stabilizing the satellite.

Outcomes

GASPACS completed successfully completed both objectives.
