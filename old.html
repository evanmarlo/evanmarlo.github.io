GASPACS

The Get Away Special Passive Attitude Control Satellite (GASPACS) was 1U CubeSat created by undergraduate students, including myself. It was the first satellite created entirely by undergraduate students and the first satellite to use a Raspberry Pi as its onboard computer (we even got a shoutout from Raspberry Pi).

I started working on the satellite's open source software in Fall of 2020 and was hired by the Physics department as a Summer Fellow in 2021. I primarily worked on driver protection and transmission protection software in Python. I also worked on unit tests for the flight software.

The satellite was a technology demonstration, testing an inflatable structure in low Earth orbit. The structure purpose was to stabilize the satellite. GASPACS completed both of its objectives: delpoying an inflatable aeroboom and transmitting a picture back down, as well as determining the boom's effectiveness at passively stabilizing the satellite.
